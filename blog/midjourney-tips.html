Advanced Parameters Deep Dive

MidJourney offers powerful parameters for precise control. Here are the most useful advanced parameters:

Parameter Description Example Best For
--chaos Controls variation between results (0-100) --chaos 75 Creative exploration, unexpected results
--stylize Adjusts artistic interpretation (0-1000) --stylize 600 Balancing realism vs. artistic flair
--weird Pushes boundaries of surrealism (0-3000) --weird 1500 Experimental, abstract creations
--tile Creates seamless repeating patterns --tile Textures, wallpapers, fabric designs
--no Excludes unwanted elements --no text, watermark Removing common artifacts
--quality Adjusts rendering time/detail (.25-2) --quality 1.5 When fine details matter

Parameter Combinations

Advanced users often combine parameters for specific effects:

Example Combination:
/imagine prompt: Cyberpunk street market at night --chaos 40 --stylize 700 --weird 800 --v 6

This creates a balanced mix of controlled variation with strong artistic interpretation and mild surrealism.

Controlling Composition

Professional artists use specific techniques to guide image composition:

Camera Angles

Use terms like:

  • aerial view
  • low angle shot
  • extreme closeup
  • Dutch angle
portrait of a warrior, low angle shot, looking up at camera

Rule of Thirds

Guide placement with:

  • subject in left third
  • negative space on right
  • horizon on lower third
landscape photo, mountains in left third, lake reflecting sunset

Layering Elements

Structure scenes with:

  • foreground: [elements]
  • midground: [elements]
  • background: [elements]
foreground: blooming cherry tree branches | midground: stone bridge | background: misty mountains

Style Weighting

Control influence of each style using double colons:

style of Van Gogh::2 and Picasso::1.5 and Monet::1

This gives Van Gogh's style the strongest influence (2:1.5:1 ratio)

Harnessing Chaos Creatively

The --chaos parameter is one of MidJourney's most powerful tools when used strategically:

Low Chaos (0-30)

  • Predictable, similar results
  • Best for precise commercial work
  • Minimal variation between grids

Medium Chaos (40-70)

  • Balanced creativity and control
  • Good for most artistic projects
  • Noticeable but coherent variations

High Chaos (80-100)

  • Extremely varied results
  • Best for inspiration generation
  • May produce unusable outputs

Pro Tip

Start with --chaos 50, then generate variations at ¬±20 intervals to find your ideal balance for each project.

Precision Prompt Weighting

MidJourney's weighting system (::) lets you emphasize or de-emphasize elements:

Basic Weighting

red roses::1.5 white lilies::1 green leaves::0.8

Roses will be most prominent in composition

Negative Weighting

sunny beach::1 rain::-0.5

Actively reduces rainy elements in sunny scene

Complex Balancing

(medieval castle:1.3):: (futuristic lights:0.7):: (fog:0.5)::

Precise control over multiple elements

Professional Workflow

Advanced users follow this systematic approach:

1

Discovery Phase

Generate 20-30 concepts with high chaos (70-90) to explore possibilities

2

Refinement Phase

Select 3-5 promising concepts and regenerate with medium chaos (40-60)

3

Precision Phase

Use seeds and low chaos (10-30) to perfect chosen concepts

4

Variation Phase

Create slight variations (lighting, angles) from final seeds

5

Post-Processing

Upscale favorites and edit externally (Photoshop, etc.)
